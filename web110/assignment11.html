Lesson Summary

Computer graphics are pictures and films created using computers. The development of computer graphics has revolutionized many types of media, including animation, movies, advertising, video games, and graphic design.

Computer animation is the art of creating moving images via the use of computers. This has opened up new possibilities for visual storytelling and entertainment.

A raster graphics or bitmap image is a dot matrix data structure representing a rectangular grid of pixels or points of color. Raster graphics are resolution dependent and cannot scale up without loss of quality.

Vector graphics use polygons to represent images in computer graphics. Vector art is ideal for printing since it is made from mathematical curves and retains crispness even when resized.

Raster-based image editors, such as Painter, Photoshop, Paint.NET, MS Paint, and GIMP, focus on editing pixels. On the other hand, vector-based image editors, such as Xfig, CorelDRAW, Adobe Illustrator, or Inkscape, focus on editing lines and shapes.

The <img> element in HTML is used to insert an image into a web page. It requires the src attribute to specify the image URL and the alt attribute to provide alternative text in case the image cannot be displayed.

Multimedia combines different content forms such as text, audio, images, animations, video, and interactive content. It can be recorded and played on various devices, enabling rich and immersive experiences.

Streaming media delivers multimedia content in real-time to end-users, allowing them to start playing the content before it has fully downloaded. Live streaming refers to Internet content delivered as events happen.

Enhanced levels of interactivity in multimedia are achieved by combining multiple forms of media content. This allows for object-oriented and data-driven applications with collaborative end-user innovation and personalization over time.

In addition to seeing and hearing, haptic technology enables users to feel virtual objects, enhancing the multimedia experience. Emerging technologies may also involve illusions of taste and smell.

Multimedia can be broadly categorized into linear and non-linear content. Linear content progresses without navigational control, such as cinema presentations, while non-linear content, like video games or self-paced computer-based training, uses interactivity to control progress.

Multimedia finds applications in various fields including advertisements, art, education, entertainment, engineering, medicine, mathematics, business, scientific research, and spatial-temporal applications.
